1. How do we operate?

By processing farm produce fresh off the farm into easy storage, ready to use final products and marketing them directly to out end customers, we offer quality assurance for every pack of Ere-Oko foods purchased by the customer.

2. Who are the farmers and where are is the food grown?

We therefore work with local farmers who are already into farming the land on a subsistent or semi-commercial farming. We first give these farmers additional training and education in innovative new farming techniques before we get to business. Our farms are situated in Teseyin, Ijebu-Igbo area of Ogun state.

3. Are Organic Products Of The Same Quality As Other Food And Drink?

Organic food and drink are of the same and even higher quality than other foods. They are consciously grown to be free of possible harmful chemicals or fertilizers which might affect size but limit taste and nutrition of the food.

4. Do Organic Products Taste Different From Other Food And Drink?

Most of the older generation mothers often differentiate foods based on ‘agric’ and ‘oko’. These words differentiated the artificially fertilized foods, with big often shiny and attractive produce, from the ‘oko’ or locally grown often smaller, less glossy food variety. The locally grown foods, though smaller and sometimes more expensive were certified tastier than the artificial fertilized variety. This is why ere-oko foods is especially particular on natural fertilizers and sustainable agricultural practices that produce great-tasting ‘oko’ foods.

5. What is the difference between organic and natural food?

While non-organic food might have been grown with the use of artificially made chemical fertilizers, genetically, it is still made up of the same genes as the organic food, hence it is natural. But with the introduction of genetically altered/engineered plants, there is a need to differentiate between plants that grow naturally and those that have been engineered to be a certain way. Ere oko foods are both ORGANIC and NATURAL Foods.
